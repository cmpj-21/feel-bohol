Key Areas of Bohol

City

Tagbilaran City

The capital and main entry point. Home to Baclayon Church, the Sandugo Monument, the airport, ferry terminals, and most hotels. Start your island journey here.

Island

Panglao Island

Connected to the mainland by bridges, Panglao is Bohol's beach capital. Alona Beach, Dumaluan Beach, Hinagdanan Cave, and Balicasag Island boat trips are all based here.

Interior

Carmen / Batuan / Sagbayan

The heart of Bohol's countryside. The Chocolate Hills viewpoint in Carmen is the most popular, but Sagbayan Peak offers a quieter, equally stunning vantage point.

River Town

Loboc & Corella

The Loboc River cruise departs from Loboc town. Nearby Corella is home to the Philippine Tarsier Sanctuary. The two stops are natural companions on the countryside loop.

Hidden Gem

Candijay & Anda

The east coast offers Bohol's best-kept secrets: Cadapdapan Rice Terraces, Can-umantad Falls (Bohol's tallest), Lamanoc Island's cave art, and pristine Anda Beach.

Night Tour

Abatan River

After dark, the Abatan River near Cortes transforms into a magical corridor of wild fireflies. Small bancas glide silently through the mangroves as thousands of lights blink overhead.

Getting There

How to Reach Bohol

Direct flights from Manila (1h 20m), Cebu (25m), and seasonal routes from Clark and Davao. The airport is a 10-minute drive from the city centre and 30 minutes from Panglao.
Frequent fast ferries run from Cebu City (Pier 1) to Tagbilaran Port (2 hours) and Tubigon Port (1.5 hours). Operators include OceanJet, SuperCat, and Lite Shipping. Book ahead during peak season.
Van Rental (with driver): ₱4,000–₱4,500 per 8-hour countryside tour — the most convenient option.

Self-Drive Van: ₱3,000–₱4,000 per 24 hours.

Scooter: ₱400–₱600 per day. Ideal for Panglao island-hopping.

Tricycle: Short hops within towns. Negotiate the fare before boarding.
