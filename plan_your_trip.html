When to Visit

Best Season

Peak Season

December – May

The dry season. Sunny skies, calm seas, ideal beach and diving conditions. January to May is when the Chocolate Hills turn their signature chocolate-brown. Book accommodation early — this fills fast.

Value Season

June – November

The rainy season brings lush, emerald-green Chocolate Hills and fewer crowds. Accommodation rates drop significantly. Outdoor activities are still possible between rain showers. Typhoon watch advised Aug–Oct.

Festivals

July — Sandugo Festival

If you can make it in July, the month-long Sandugo Festival fills Tagbilaran with street dancing, reenactments, and pageants. Book months in advance as hotels fill up region-wide.

Itinerary

Sample 3-Day Countryside + Beach Route

Arrive at Tagbilaran Airport or Port. Check into your hotel. Afternoon: visit Baclayon Church and the nearby Blood Compact Shrine. Evening: explore Tagbilaran's downtown — try local calamay, broas, and peanut kisses from the market.
Morning: Drive the Bilar Manmade Forest. Stop at Corella for the Tarsier Sanctuary.

Midday: Loboc River Cruise — two hours of floating buffet and cultural shows on the emerald river.

Afternoon: Head to Carmen for the Chocolate Hills viewpoint (sunrise/sunset views are best). Return via Batuan.

Tip: Hire a van with driver (₱4,000–4,500) to cover this loop in one day comfortably.
Morning: Island-hop to Balicasag Island for world-class snorkelling and sea turtle encounters.

Afternoon: Return to Alona Beach — lunch, rest, or a second dive. Explore Hinagdanan Cave in the late afternoon.

Evening: Sunset beers on Alona Beach. Dinner at one of the beachside restaurants.
Day 4: Abatan River firefly watching night tour.

Day 5: East Bohol — Cadapdapan Rice Terraces and Can-umantad Falls (Bohol's tallest waterfall).

Day 6: Binabaje Hills hike (Alicia) — stunning ridgeline views.

Day 7: Lamanoc Island cave art (Anda) + Anda beach relaxation.

What to Bring

Packing Tips

Essential

Sun & Sea

Reef-safe sunscreen (required at some marine sanctuaries), rash guard, underwater camera, snorkel gear (or rent on-site), and a dry bag for boat trips.

Practical

Cash First

Many smaller establishments outside Tagbilaran and Panglao are cash-only. Withdraw at Tagbilaran ATMs on arrival — GCash is increasingly accepted in tourist areas.

Respect

Wildlife Etiquette

No touching tarsiers. No flash photography. Keep voices low at the sanctuary. At coral reefs, no touching or standing on coral. These rules protect what makes Bohol special.
