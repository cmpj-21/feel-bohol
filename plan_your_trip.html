When to Visit

Best Season

Peak Season

December – May

The dry season brings sunny skies, calmer seas, and ideal beach and diving conditions. January to May is when the Chocolate Hills turn their signature chocolate brown, so book early if those months are your priority.

Value Season

June – November

The wetter season turns the hills lush green and thins out the crowds. Outdoor days are still very possible between showers, but August to October usually need the most weather flexibility.

Festivals

July — Sandugo Festival

If you travel in July, Tagbilaran becomes far livelier with the Sandugo Festival. Expect heavier demand for stays and transport, but also the strongest cultural atmosphere on the island.

What to Bring

Packing Tips

Essential

Sun & Sea

Bring reef-safe sunscreen, a rash guard, sandals that can get wet, and a dry bag for island-hopping days. If snorkeling is high on your list, it is still worth packing your own mask for a better fit.

Practical

Cash First

Many smaller establishments outside Tagbilaran and Panglao still lean cash-first. Withdraw on arrival and keep smaller bills for tricycles, food stalls, environmental fees, and local guides.

Respect

Wildlife Etiquette

No touching tarsiers, no flash photography, and no standing on coral. The island experience is better when visitors protect the fragile wildlife and reef systems that make Bohol special.
