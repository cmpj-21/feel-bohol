## History & Heritage

### The Sandugo (Blood Compact)

- **Origin:** Occurred on March 25, 1565, in Tagbilaran.
- **Significance:** A treaty of friendship between Datu Sikatuna (local chieftain) and Miguel López de Legazpi (Spanish explorer). It symbolizes the first international treaty of friendship between Filipinos and Spaniards.
- **Commemoration:** The annual **Sandugo Festival** in July features street dancing, reenactments, and the Miss Bohol pageant.

### The Dagohoy Rebellion (1744–1829)

- **Leader:** Francisco Dagohoy (Sendrijas).
- **Significance:** The longest rebellion in Philippine history, lasting **85 years**.
- **Cause:** Sparked by the refusal of a Jesuit priest to give a Christian burial to Dagohoy's brother.
- **Legacy:** Established an independent community in the mountains that successfully resisted Spanish rule for generations.

### Religious Heritage: Baclayon Church

- **Foundation:** Established in 1596 by Jesuits; the current coral stone structure was completed in 1727.
- **Status:** National Cultural Treasure and National Historical Landmark.
- **Fact:** Built using coral stones bound together with egg whites as mortar.
- **Museum:** Features a rich collection of religious artifacts and antique vestments.

## Traveler's Guide & Logistics

### Best Time to Visit

- **Peak Season (Dec – May):** Best weather, sunny skies, calm seas.
- **Iconic View:** January to May is when the Chocolate Hills turn their signature brown.
- **Value Season (June – Nov):** Lush green landscapes, fewer crowds, lower accommodation rates.

### Transport Options (2026)

- **Van Rental (w/ Driver):** ₱4,000 – ₱4,500 for an 8-hour countryside tour.
- **Self-Drive Van:** ₱3,000 – ₱4,000 per 24 hours.
- **Scooter Rental:** ₱400 – ₱600 per day (popular in Panglao).

## Flavors of Bohol (Local Delicacies)

- **Calamay (Jagna):** A sweet, sticky treat made from glutinous rice and coconut milk, traditionally served in coconut shells.
- **Peanut Kisses:** Crunchy, hill-shaped cookies that mimic the Chocolate Hills.
- **Broas:** Traditional ladyfingers, a specialty of Baclayon.
- **Ube Kinampay:** A premium variety of purple yam distinctive to Bohol, celebrated in the **Ubi Festival** (January).
- **Hillcolate:** Specialized Boholano chocolate products incorporating local flavors.

## Hidden Gems (For 2026)

- **Cadapdapan Rice Terraces:** A serene alternative to the more crowded terraces in the north.
- **Can-umantad Falls:** Bohol's tallest waterfall, located in Candijay.
- **Binabaje Hills (Alicia):** Known as "Little Switzerland" for its stunning ridgeline hiking trails.
- **Lamanoc Island (Anda):** A mystical site featuring pre-colonial burial caves and rock art.
- **Abatan River Firefly Watching:** A magical night-time tour through mangrove forests.
